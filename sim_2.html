The Wilson Cowan Model





What is the Wilson-Cowan Model?

Glad you asked! It is a computation that is designed to explain and represent the interaction between neural populations such that they are broken down into two types: Inhibitory and Excitatory. Inhibition is the lack of neuron activity in a neural population and Excitatory is the opposite, the presence of activity. In the WC(Wilson-Cowan) model, a subtractive(or addition) operation is computed to simulate these reactions. Needless to say, there are more factors and operations at play such that they are firing rates, functions representing neuron transfer, and synaptic weights. Sources will be cited below.

Computation:

[(dE/dt) - (dI/dt)] would represent the overall computation of an Inhibitory population combined with an Excitory one.

  • Excitatory rate with respect to miliseconds: [dE/dt] = -E + F(WEEE - WEII + Iext)

    WEE, WEI are synaptic weights measured in milivolts or dimensionless.

    Iext expresses external input.

    F is the transfer function representing population response.

    E is the firing rate

  • Inibitory rate with respect to miliseconds[dI/dt] = -I + F(WIE)E - WIII + Iext

    WIE, WII: synaptic weights.

    Iext expresses external input.

    F: transfer function.

    I is the firing rate.
